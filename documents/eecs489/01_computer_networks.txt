EECS 489 is Computer Networks. Public EECS 489 course materials describe it as a class about how networks operate and how network applications are written.

The course description says students study the workings of Ethernet and the Internet: how packets are routed, how packets are transmitted, and what to do when there is network congestion. It also mentions packet headers, routing and transmission protocols, sockets, and writing code.

Another public course repository describes a top-down approach covering popular distributed systems such as video streaming, content distribution networks, and cloud computing systems, plus transport layer protocols such as TCP and UDP.

For course selection, EECS 489 is a strong fit for students interested in networking, Internet architecture, distributed systems basics, sockets, routing, congestion, TCP/UDP, and client-server or peer-to-peer applications. It is more network-specific than EECS 482 and more infrastructure-focused than EECS 485.
