EECS 281 is Data Structures and Algorithms at the University of Michigan. The course homepage labels it as Data Structures and Algorithms in C++. The course is a major step after EECS 280 because it moves from introductory data structures into algorithmic analysis, graph algorithms, sorting, hashing, trees, dynamic programming, and optimization.

The public schedule includes topics such as stack, queue, and priority queue ADTs; complexity analysis and math foundations; recursion and the Master Theorem; heaps and heapsort; ordered arrays; elementary sorts; quicksort; mergesort; hashing; trees; binary search trees; AVL trees; graph ADTs; minimum spanning tree algorithms; algorithm families; backtracking and branch and bound for traveling salesperson; dynamic programming; knapsack; greedy single-source algorithms; strings and sequences; and computational geometry if time permits.

The course homepage includes a projects section. The projects emphasize graph search and route tracing with BFS and DFS; priority queues, templated containers, inheritance, interface programming, and streaming algorithms; hash tables and larger data structures through composition; and optimization algorithms such as traveling salesperson and knapsack. This makes EECS 281 a course for students who want algorithms with substantial programming projects.

For course selection, EECS 281 is important for students who want the core CS sequence and need preparation for many upper-level electives. It is a better match for students who are ready for algorithmic thinking and C++ implementation than for students looking for a lighter conceptual elective.
